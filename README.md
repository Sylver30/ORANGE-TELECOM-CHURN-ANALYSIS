This Telecom Churn Analysis examines customer data from Orange Telecom to understand the factors that influence why customers leave the service. The analysis focuses mainly on customer service interactions, subscription plans, and usage patterns to identify behaviours that are associated with a higher likelihood of churn. The study seeks to identify patterns that differentiate loyal customers from those who churn. The insights generated can help telecom companies improve customer retention strategies, enhance service quality and design better pricing and that of service plans. 


Objectives


1)	Customer Service Performance as a Predictor of Churn
2)	Plan subscription and usage as drivers of churn 

Research questions 
1)	What causes does frequent service complaints lead to? 
2)	What is the correlation between customer service calls and churn?
3)	What tipping points does Churn customers have?
4)	What is the sum of total day charge and international charge by churn? 


KEY PERFORMANCE INDICATORS (KPIs)
1)	Churn Rate
2)	Average churn per user 
3)	Sum of customer service calls 

In the determining if frequent service complaints lead to churn, customers with 5 plus (5+) calls usually show higher churn probability. It as well indicates customer dissatisfaction. Frequent service complaints are a major churn predictor. The correlation between customer service calls and churn. This measures the relation strength. It is observed that frustration increases with repeated support interactions. Churn sharply increases after four (4) service calls. Support teams must resolve issues before customers reach this threshold. 

Geographic analysis of service issues 
This identifies problematic regions. Some states show high complaint frequency and churn levels. It can be corrected by better customer support staffing. 

International Plan versus churn rate Customers with international plan tend to churn more due to higher billing charges. Revenue versus churn (daily and international charges) 
Churned customers often have higher usage costs. High bills can trigger churn due to price sensitivity. Voice mail plan versus customer retention – Customers with voice mail plan churn less. Voice mail plan acts as a customer retention feature.    

Executive Insights 
1)	Customer service complaints strongly predict churn
2)	Three plus service calls are the tipping point
3)	International plan customers churn more 
4)	Higher billing charges increase churn risk
5)	Voice mail plan improves retention
